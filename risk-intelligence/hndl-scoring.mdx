## How scoring works

The HNDL threat model is straightforward: adversaries are harvesting encrypted traffic today with the intent to decrypt it once a cryptographically relevant quantum computer (CRQC) becomes available. The risk of a given asset depends on four factors: how sensitive the data it protects is, how soon a CRQC might arrive, how exposed the asset is to potential harvesters, and how urgently compliance frameworks require remediation.

Quanterios scores each dimension from 0 to 25, then sums them for a total score of 0 to 100.

## Scoring dimensions

| Dimension | Max score | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Data Sensitivity** | 25 | The value and sensitivity of the data this asset protects. Health records, financial data, and government secrets score highest. Internal tooling scores lowest. |
| **Quantum Timeline** | 25 | How much time remains before a CRQC could break this algorithm, relative to the data's required confidentiality window. Long-lived data protected by RSA-2048 scores highest. |
| **Exposure Surface** | 25 | How accessible the encrypted traffic is to a harvesting adversary. Internet-facing TLS endpoints score highest. Air-gapped internal services score lowest. |
| **Regulatory Pressure** | 25 | The urgency of applicable compliance deadlines. Assets in scope for CNSA 2.0 or NIS2 with near-term deadlines score highest. |

## Severity mapping

| Score range | Severity | Recommended action |
|---|---|---|
| 80-100 | **Critical** | Migrate immediately. Include in next board report. |
| 60-79 | **High** | Schedule migration within current quarter. |
| 40-59 | **Medium** | Include in next annual migration cycle. |
| 20-39 | **Low** | Monitor. Prioritize when capacity allows. |
| 0-19 | **Informational** | Acceptable risk. No immediate action required. |

## Dimension breakdown

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Data Sensitivity (0-25)">
    This dimension reflects the real-world value of the data the asset protects. Quanterios infers sensitivity from asset metadata, tags, and the services the asset is attached to.

    | Score range | Sensitivity level | Examples |
    |---|---|---|
    | 20-25 | Highly sensitive | Healthcare records (HIPAA), cardholder data (PCI), classified government data |
    | 13-19 | Sensitive | PII, financial records, intellectual property |
    | 6-12 | Internal | Internal APIs, employee systems, business data |
    | 0-5 | Public or low-risk | Public-facing static content, development environments |

    You can override the inferred sensitivity level on any asset using the `data_sensitivity` field in the CBOM API.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Quantum Timeline (0-25)">
    This dimension models the intersection of two timelines: when a CRQC capable of breaking the asset's algorithm is expected to arrive, and how long the data protected by this asset needs to remain confidential.

    Quanterios uses published estimates from NIST, BSI, and ANSSI for CRQC timelines. These estimates are updated as new research emerges.

    | Algorithm | Current quantum vulnerability |
    |---|---|
    | RSA-2048 | Breakable by a CRQC with ~4000 logical qubits (Shor's algorithm) |
    | ECDSA P-256 | Breakable by a CRQC with ~2000 logical qubits |
    | AES-128 | Security halved by Grover's algorithm; still adequate for most uses |
    | AES-256 | Grover-resistant at current threat levels |
    | ML-KEM / ML-DSA | Post-quantum safe; scores 0 on this dimension |
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Exposure Surface (0-25)">
    This dimension measures how easily an adversary can observe and harvest the encrypted traffic this asset protects.

    | Score range | Surface | Description |
    |---|---|---|
    | 20-25 | Internet-facing | TLS endpoints reachable by the public internet |
    | 13-19 | Network-accessible | Internal services accessible from broad corporate networks |
    | 6-12 | Restricted | Services behind VPN, private subnets, or network ACLs |
    | 0-5 | Air-gapped or local | Encryption at rest on disks, local-only services |

    Quanterios infers exposure from connector metadata (e.g. whether an ELB is internet-facing, whether a CloudFront distribution is public). You can override exposure level per asset.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Regulatory Pressure (0-25)">
    This dimension reflects the urgency of compliance deadlines applicable to this asset. An asset in scope for a framework with a deadline 6 months away scores higher than the same asset with a deadline 4 years away.

    Quanterios maps each asset to the applicable compliance frameworks based on your organization profile and asset metadata, then computes a deadline urgency score across all applicable frameworks.

    See the [Compliance Engine](/risk-intelligence/compliance) for the full list of supported frameworks and how assets are mapped.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## AI-generated briefings

For every asset with an HNDL score above 40, Quanterios generates a plain-language AI briefing explaining the risk in terms a non-technical executive can understand. Briefings include:

- A one-paragraph summary of why this asset is at risk
- The specific algorithm weakness and its quantum vulnerability
- The compliance frameworks it affects
- A recommended next step

Briefings are generated using the [HNDL API](/api-reference/hndl) and are included automatically in PDF board reports.
