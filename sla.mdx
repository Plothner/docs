<Note>

</Note>

## API Availability

Quanterios targets **99.9% monthly uptime** for `api.quanterios.com`.

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Monthly uptime | 99.9% |
| Measurement window | Calendar month |
| Measurement method | External synthetic monitoring |
| Excluded | Planned maintenance, force majeure |

Uptime is calculated as: `(total minutes - downtime minutes) / total minutes * 100`. Downtime begins when our monitoring detects two consecutive failed health checks at `api.quanterios.com/healthz`.

## Planned Maintenance

Planned maintenance is announced at least **48 hours in advance** via the status page at `api.quanterios.com/healthz` and by email to the account owner.

Maintenance windows are scheduled between **02:00 and 06:00 UTC on weekdays** to minimize impact for EU and US business hours. Maintenance periods are excluded from uptime calculations.

## Incident Response

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Severity definitions">
    | Severity | Definition |
    |----------|------------|
    | P1 | `api.quanterios.com` is completely unreachable or returns 5xx for more than 50% of requests |
    | P2 | Core functionality is degraded: assessment, migration, or firewall endpoints return errors or latency exceeds 10 seconds |
    | P3 | Non-critical features are impaired; workarounds exist; no data loss |
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Response time commitments">
    | Severity | First response | Status update cadence | Target resolution |
    |----------|---------------|----------------------|-------------------|
    | P1 (service down) | 1 hour | Every 30 minutes | 4 hours |
    | P2 (degraded) | 4 hours | Every 2 hours | 24 hours |
    | P3 (minor) | 24 hours | As needed | Next release |

    Response times are measured from the time the incident is confirmed by Quanterios monitoring or reported via a support channel listed below.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Incident communication">
    Incident status is published in real time at `api.quanterios.com/healthz`. For P1 and P2 incidents, the account owner receives email updates at each status change. Post-incident reviews are published within five business days of resolution for P1 incidents.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Status and Health Check

The health endpoint is publicly accessible and returns a JSON payload with current service status:

```bash
curl https://api.quanterios.com/healthz
```

```json
{
  "status": "ok",
  "api": "ok",
  "database": "ok",
  "airflow": "ok",
  "timestamp": "2026-04-18T10:00:00Z"
}
```

<Info>
  You can configure external monitoring (Datadog, Uptime Robot, Grafana Cloud) to poll `api.quanterios.com/healthz` directly. The endpoint requires no authentication.
</Info>

## Support Channels

| Channel | Plans | Response target |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| Email: support@quanterios.com | All paid plans | P3: 24 hours |
| GitHub Issues | All plans | Best effort |
| Dedicated Slack channel | Enterprise | P1: 1 hour, P2: 4 hours |

Enterprise customers receive a dedicated Slack Connect channel shared with the Quanterios engineering team. P1 and P2 incidents can be escalated directly in Slack with an acknowledgment within 15 minutes during business hours (09:00 to 18:00 CET).

## SLA Credits

If monthly uptime falls below 99.9%, paid customers are eligible for service credits:

| Monthly uptime | Credit |
|----------------|--------|
| 99.0% to 99.9% | 10% of monthly fee |
| 95.0% to 99.0% | 25% of monthly fee |
| Below 95.0% | 50% of monthly fee |

Credits are applied to the next billing cycle. To claim a credit, email support@quanterios.com within 30 days of the incident with the relevant dates and affected tenant ID.
